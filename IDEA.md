# Mockingbird — The Tone Trainer

The first tool in the [wider toolkit](VISION.md). It imitates, and it makes you imitate.

---

## The thesis

Five commitments, each one a place existing tools usually fail:

1. **Ear before mouth.** You cannot produce a contrast you can't hear. Perception first.
2. **Relative, not absolute.** A tone is a *shape* in your own vocal range, not a target frequency. Calibrate to the learner; never punish a deep or high voice.
3. **Diagnose, don't score.** "73%" teaches nothing. "Your second tone doesn't rise steeply enough" teaches everything.
4. **Meaning always attached.** Every drill item is a real word with a character and a consequence. Tones exist to carry meaning; practice should too.
5. **Graduate into real speech, then fade.** Isolated syllables are the easy, least-useful part. The goal is connected speech — and then to disappear, like posture for a musician.

---

## The steps

These double as the learner's path *and* the build order.

**Step 0 — Calibrate.** Record a few sustained sounds, map the learner's pitch range. Everything downstream is scored relative to *their* register. (Fixes the single most common false-negative in pitch tools.)

**Step 1 — Ear training, no speaking yet.** Adaptive *discrimination* ("same or different?") and *identification* ("which tone?") drills, using many native voices — male, female, child, regional — so the skill attaches to the tone, not to one speaker. An adaptive engine hunts the learner's specific blind spot (almost always tone 2 vs. tone 3) instead of drilling everything evenly. **Gate:** don't unlock production until perception is reliable.

**Step 2 — The pitch mirror.** You speak; your voice draws a glowing line in real time against a ghost target contour, normalized to your range. Karaoke for pitch. **Built-in ceiling:** once you're "good enough" on a syllable, it stops letting you camp there. Perfectionism in isolation is a trap.

**Step 3 — Diagnostic coaching.** Not a percentage — a named fault and a fix. *"Your T3 is fine alone but collapses at sentence end — hold the low part longer."* *"Start your T4 higher so the fall has somewhere to go."* This is the magic-wand layer: the intelligence that knows *how* you're wrong, which is the one thing software almost never delivers and a human tutor always does.

**Step 4 — Meaning, with consequences.** Every item is a real word + character + sense. Say *mǎ* (horse) when you meant *mā* (mother) and a horse trots onto the screen, or your character walks up to the wrong person. The cost of the error becomes *visible* and a little funny — so it sticks.

**Step 5 — Tone pairs (the bridge).** Move to two-syllable combinations — the 20-cell pair matrix — because that's how the language actually chunks. Front-load the killers (3-3, 4-1, 3-2). Teach sandhi *implicitly* through imitation, not rules: the learner just hears and copies that two third-tones come out as rising + dipping, and the pattern installs itself. **Don't dump all 20 at once** — that reliably craters learners; feed a few rows at a time.

**Step 6 — Phrases and sentences.** Shadowing real native audio. Here tones warp on purpose — coarticulation drags one into the next, T3 flattens mid-sentence. Feedback shifts from "perfect contour" to "natural rhythm + intelligible." Useful truth baked in: the importance of any single tone is *inversely proportional to how predictable the sentence is* — context shoulders some of the load, and the tool should stop demanding studio-perfect tones once you're understood.

**Step 7 — Maintenance.** Spaced review of your weak tones, pairs, and words; periodic re-diagnosis to catch backsliding. The endgame is invisibility — tones drop out of conscious attention the way stance does for a fighter.

---

## Why this isn't just a reskin of what exists

The individual pieces are scattered across today's apps — pitch graphs and speech scoring in one, native audio in another, pair drills in a third, human feedback in a fourth. **Nothing knits perception → diagnosis → production → connected speech into one adaptive loop driven by a real model of where *your* ear and mouth are failing.** That connective intelligence is the whole point.
